Classification Guidelines: What Constitutes “AI for Evil”
(Preliminary Ideas, Must Be Amended As the Project Progresses)
1. Information and Perception Manipulation
A. Automated Disinformation Systems
AI systems that generate or amplify false or misleading information at scale.
* Content is intentionally fabricated or misleading
* Automation significantly increases distribution capacity
* Designed to influence public opinion, elections, or social stability
B. Synthetic Media Deception
AI systems that produce realistic fake audio, video, images, or documents.
* Impersonation without consent
* High realism intended to deceive
C. Narrative Amplification Engines
Algorithms optimized to maximize outrage, division, or polarization.
* Objective function prioritizes emotional intensity over accuracy
* Evidence of increased ideological clustering or extremity


2. Exploitation and Manipulation
A. Predatory Targeting Systems
AI that identifies and exploits vulnerable individuals (def. of vulnerable must be decided).
* Behavioral profiling used for extraction (possible biased datasets)
* Adaptive manipulation strategies
B. Addiction Optimization Systems (Likely a gray area that may be removed from guidelines)
Recommender systems designed to maximize compulsive engagement.
* Optimization for time spent without regard for well-being
* Escalation toward more extreme or stimulating content
* Reinforcement loop design
C. Financial Extraction Algorithms
AI systems used to maximize debt, fees, or economic dependency.
* Dynamic pricing targeting specific groups
* Intentional differences in provided information
* Optimization for maximum profit extraction at the expense of targeted populations


3. Surveillance and Control
A. Mass Surveillance Systems
AI used for large-scale monitoring without consent.
* Biometric or behavioral tracking at population scale
* Lack of transparency or oversight
* Data aggregation for control purposes
B. Predictive Suppression Systems
AI used to predict and suppress dissent or behavior.
* Automated scoring and profiling tied to penalties or restrictions of freedoms
* Deployment in political or coercive contexts
C. Social Scoring Mechanisms
Systems that assign behavioral or loyalty scores affecting access to services.
* (Criteria Currently Undecided)


4. Cyber and Infrastructure Harm
A. Automated Cyberattack Tools
AI systems that identify and exploit digital vulnerabilities.
* Automated vulnerability scanning and exploitation
* Designed for unauthorized system access
* Scalable attack capacity
B. Infrastructure Disruption Systems
AI capable of targeting power grids, hospitals, or transportation systems.
* Ability to interfere with critical services
* Potential for large-scale societal disruption (subjective interpretation)
* Limited reversibility of impact (hard to undo)
C. Autonomous Weaponization
AI used for targeting, coordination, or deployment of physical force.
* Lethal or destructive capacity
* Reduced human oversight
* Automated target selection




5. Institutional and Structural Manipulation
A. Metric Gaming Systems
AI designed to improve reported performance while hurting real outcomes and consumers.
* (Criteria Currently Undecided)
B. Market Manipulation Systems (Likely a gray area that may be removed from guidelines)
AI used to distort financial markets or extract value unfairly.
* Coordinated artificial price movement
* Exploiting differences in knowledge
C. Accountability Evasion Systems
AI systems designed to conceal operator identity or decision logic.
* Deliberate opacity
* Difficult-to-understand responsibility structures
* Technical barriers to auditing or reviewing
